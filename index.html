In digital marketing, Facebook and Google are known as walled gardens. By aggressively centralizing and guarding vast amounts of audience data behind closed doors, they secure the bulk of the attention economy. The valued commodity behind these walls is us — habituated, surveilled, weary-eyed.

Open Garden is a durational participatory performance by artist Alice Yuan Zhang, experimenting with the potentiality of our online attention and the labor of digital sovereignty. A garden plot is livestreamed by solar power to the distributed web. Instructions are freely negotiated by site visitors throughout each week, then carried out by the artist. Over an initial course of a month, Open Garden acts as substrate for active reflection.

Can we begin to decommodify our attention and reclaim digital agency? Where might we direct that attention, and what grows from it? How do we negotiate individual instincts with collective responsibility? What does it mean to govern ourselves in consideration of pace, place, and perspective?








about the garden

This particular plot is on unceded Tongva, Chumash, and Kizh land. Weather here is generally dry and sunny in the Spring, with daytime temperatures ranging from 70-80° F and evenings at around 50-60° F. The garden's fate will change based on the living assemblage of instructions generated here each week, as well as the moderation of the artist. The initial course of this project will run for a month or longer.








about the network

You are currently on the Beaker browser, which runs the Hypercore protocol. Hypercore (hyper://) is a peer-to-peer network where data is held and exchanged between site visitor only. Instead of a central server such as Amazon Web Services, we each can have a direct role in keeping this site live.

Simply click on in the top right corner, then toggle "Host this Hyperdrive". This makes you a node of the network of people hosting Open Garden. Now, whenever you are here, new visitors will be also be able to access the garden. The more people we have hosting together, the more likely the site will be accessible at any given time.



Open Garden is designed and performed by CultureHub resident artist Alice Yuan Zhang for Re-Fest 2021: "Re-Circulation". Alice Yuan Zhang (she/her) is a first-generation Chinese-American artist, educator, and community organizer, using new media technology to facilitate collaborative engagement around ecology, networks, and home.
