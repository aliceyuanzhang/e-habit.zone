In digital marketing, Facebook and Google are known as walled gardens. By aggressively centralizing and guarding vast amounts of audience data behind closed doors, they secure the bulk of the attention economy. The valued commodity behind these walls is us — habituated, surveilled, weary-eyed.

Open Garden is a durational participatory performance by artist Alice Yuan Zhang, experimenting with the potentiality of our online attention and the labor of digital sovereignty. A garden plot is livestreamed by solar power to the distributed web. Instructions are freely negotiated by site visitors throughout each week, then carried out by the artist. Over an initial course of a month, Open Garden acts as substrate for active reflection.

Can we begin to decommodify our attention and reclaim digital agency? Where might we direct that attention, and what grows from it? How do we negotiate individual instincts with collective responsibility? What does it mean to govern ourselves in consideration of pace, place, and perspective?








about the garden

This particular plot is on unceded Tongva, Chumash, and Kizh land. Weather here is generally dry and sunny in the Spring, with daytime temperatures ranging from 70-80° F and evenings at around 50-60° F. The garden's fate relies on the negotiation of instructions here each week, as well as the moderation and capabilities of the artist.








about the network

In a decentralized network, we directly sharing the responsibility of keep a website running, bypassing a central server like Amazon Web Services or Google Cloud Platform. Currently, files for this website are decentralized through the Beaker Browser, which runs on a peer-to-peer protocol called Hypercore (hyper://). However, messages and email addresses are still stored in a central server, which we aim to decentralize over the course of this initial run of the project.

You are invited to "seed" the website to keep Open Garden up and running:

Simply click on in the top right corner, then toggle "Host this Hyperdrive".

Now whenever you are here, you act as a node of a network of folks keeping the site live for newcomers. The more people we have seeding together, the more likely the site will be accessible at any given time. We also encourage you to seed the website when Beaker is closed. Just check your settings (visit URL “beaker://settings/”) and make sure you allow "Let Beaker run in the background".

Visit this living document to learn more about the main centralized networks we are currently entangled in, as well as our intention, process, and challenges to decentralize Open Garden step by step.



Open Garden is designed and performed by CultureHub resident artist Alice Yuan Zhang for Re-Fest 2021: "Re-Circulation". Alice Yuan Zhang (she/her) is a first-generation Chinese-American artist, educator, and community organizer, using new media technology to facilitate collaborative engagement around ecology, networks, and home.
